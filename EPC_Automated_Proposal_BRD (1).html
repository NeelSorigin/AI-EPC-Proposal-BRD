Problem Statement
⏱

Capacity calculation is specialist-gated

Only 2–3 people on the business team can size solar capacity from electricity bills. Every new AM deal queues behind them, adding days to the pipeline and blocking Anuja from working independently.

🔍

Lead qualification wastes 70% of time

AMs spend the majority of their day on Apollo/LinkedIn finding the right contact person. Over 99% of inbound inquiries are price collectors with no real intent. No AI filter exists.

📋

No structured data collection

Client information (bills, site location, existing renewables, sanctioned load) is collected ad-hoc via email, WhatsApp, and phone. No structured intake form that feeds downstream systems.

💰

Pricing approval is verbal

Every pricing decision (e.g. offering ₹330 Lakhs/MWp vs ₹340) requires a verbal meeting with the manager. No historical comparable data surfaced. Causes 1–2 day delays on live proposals.

What Execution Team Reviews at Gate 1
✓

Capacity appropriateness

Is 39.48 MWp / 28.2 MW achievable on the available land (~250 acres at Solapur)? Does DC:AC ratio of 1:1.4 match site conditions?

✓

Land profile confirmation

Is the site flat/polygon? Natural contour acceptable? Access road available? Distance from main road (~0.5km) manageable for construction logistics?

✓

Grid connectivity feasibility

Is 33kV connectivity to 220/132kV Vairag substation (~5.6km aerial) achievable within cost assumptions? ROW availability for transmission line?

⚠

Scope clarification if needed

Execution team can flag items that require revision: if capacity needs splitting (e.g. 12.18 MWp + 27.3 MWp sub-projects), this is noted here before design begins.

Problem Statement
🗺

Site layout done manually in CAD

Panel placement on satellite images is manual. Engineer opens Google Earth, traces plot boundary by hand, places module rows manually in CAD. 2–3 days per site.

📐

SLD & electrical design from scratch

String configuration, DC cable routing, inverter positions, HT yard layout all drawn from scratch in CAD for each project. No templating or AI generation.

📋

BOQ disconnected from drawings

After drawings are complete, engineer manually counts module rows, measures cable lengths, lists equipment — then hands to SCM. No automated extraction from design data.

🔗

No link between design and proposal

Satellite image, layout drawings, and GIS data exist as files, not platform data. To include in proposal, someone manually exports, resizes, and pastes into Word/PDF.

Problem Statement
💰

Repeated ad-hoc pricing queries

SCM queried manually for every proposal. 50+ cable SKUs, modules, MMS, transformer — each with location-specific pricing. No self-service KB for business team.

Problem Statement
📄

Proposal assembly is fully manual

BD team manually collects data from multiple sources (email, WhatsApp, CAD files, Excel BOQ) and types into a Word template. Formatting, typos, and outdated data are common. 1–2 days per proposal.

🔢

Proposal numbering is ad-hoc

SRESPL/YY/S/MH/042 format assigned manually. No system tracks sequence, state codes, or revision numbers. Duplicate or wrong numbers issued occasionally.

⏳

Manager pricing approval delays

Every pricing decision requires a verbal meeting. Manager has no data reference — no historical comparable deals, no win rate at different price points. Adds 1–2 day delay.

👁

No proposal tracking post-send

After proposal is emailed, no system tracks whether client has viewed it, responded, or gone silent. Shri Cement scenario: 9 months stalled, discovered only when AM manually checked.

Risk Register
Risk 1 — Critical

GIS panel placement overestimates usable area

AI-generated layout claims 39.48 MWp on 250 acres but site has obstructions (water bodies, power lines) not visible in satellite image. Underpriced proposal sent to client.

Mitigation: Execution Gate 1 & 2 both review layout feasibility. AI flags low-confidence areas on satellite image. Design engineer must confirm 250-acre usable area before Gate 2 approval.

SCM pricing staleness leads to underpriced proposal

₹330 Lakhs/MWp rate used in proposal but module prices spiked. Client accepts and Sorigin locked into loss-making contract.

Mitigation: 7-day validity window on all KB rates. Proposals using rates older than 7 days flagged in red. PV module price hike clause auto-included in commercial terms (Section 7H of Thermax proposal).
Risk 3 — Medium

Proposal auto-assembly fills wrong client data

AI pulls data from wrong NetSuite project record. Client name, site details, or pricing from another project appear in proposal.

Mitigation: BD review step shows all 10 sections in editable preview before PDF generation. Mandatory BD confirmation before proposal is generated. Project record lock during assembly.
Risk 4 — Low

ODA DWG export not available (current status: not found)

Design drawings generated but cannot be exported to DWG format for client submission.

Mitigation: PDF + SVG export as Phase 1 fallback. ODA hosting environment setup in Phase 2 Sprint 1. For first proposals, PDF format acceptable to most clients.
