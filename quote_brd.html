Business Requirements Document · v0.1

Re-Votex

An AI-augmented proposal and lead-workflow platform built for Sorigin Renewable Energy Solutions Pvt Ltd — an Indian EPC contractor delivering utility-scale solar projects today, with wind and hybrid systems on the horizon.

Owner Neel Shah · neel.shah@sorigin.co Reviewer Dhara Last updated May 13, 2026
Status — draft. Audience: internal team + external stakeholders (board, prospective enterprise clients). Items in Shipped are live in the M1 UI shell; items in Pending are scoped but not yet built. Pending Dhara's review.

1Executive summary

Sorigin Renewable Energy Solutions Pvt Ltd (SRESPL) is an Indian Engineering, Procurement, and Construction (EPC) contractor working on solar power projects. The company's deals are low-volume, high-ticket — each contract is worth crores of rupees and takes weeks of effort to win. Every customer matters.

Today, the path from a lead phone-call to a signed proposal is scattered across Excel sheets, Email threads, a CRM (Freshsales), and the team's heads. Proposals are hand-typed in Word. Each renegotiation round means rebuilding the whole document. Quotes expire in seven days — past which prices for solar modules, currency rates, and steel may have shifted — and re-costing is painful enough that some live opportunities die quietly.

Re-Votex replaces that scatter with a focused tool. It takes a lead in, runs the BD team through a guided question flow, generates a pixel-faithful techno-commercial PDF that matches Sorigin's reference proposal format, and lets the team refine the document via natural-language chat ("change the advance to 15%, OMS for 5 years") — with every edit mapped to structured fields that re-render deterministically. One click re-costs an expired proposal against current price data.

This document covers the business case, the users, the workflow, the technical architecture, the data model, the API contract, the UI surfaces, and the phased delivery roadmap. The M1 UI shell is shipped and on GitHub; the backend lands in M1.5; AI and PDF rendering land in M2; additional verticals (wind, battery storage) land in M3+.

2Business context

The shape of Sorigin's sales pipeline today, where it leaks, and where Re-Votex earns its keep.

The pipeline as it runs today

  1. Telecaller dials leads, captures intent in an Excel sheet and Freshsales.
  2. Warm leads are handed off to a Business Development (BD) team member — virtually or in person — to build trust and qualify.
  3. BD asks for documents: 12 months of electricity bills, land KYC, site photographs. These arrive over email, often lost in chat threads.
  4. BD drafts the first proposal in Word, manually computing pricing in Excel.
  5. Client negotiates — "can advance be 15% instead of 20%?", "add 5-year Operations & Maintenance Services (OMS)" — and the BD rebuilds the document round after round.
  6. The quote expires after seven days; if the client is still warm, the BD must re-cost from scratch against today's prices.
  7. Win — milestone-based payments begin. Lose — the file is filed and the lead is marked closed.
Where the pipeline leaks. Pipeline state lives in one telecaller's head; if she's out, things stall. Proposal storage is scattered across folders, Excel, and notes — there is no version history. Negotiation churn eats days of BD time per week. Quotes die at expiry because re-costing is too painful to do quickly.

Where Re-Votex helps

  1. Cuts negotiation churn. Structured proposal data + deterministic PDF render → edits take seconds, not hours.
  2. One-click re-cost. Pricing-as-a-service: update module ₹/Wp, FX rate, MMS steel rate in one place → regenerate every affected proposal.
  3. Pipeline state in the system, not someone's head. Activity sidebar and the Watchdog Agent surface stale leads, expiring proposals, callbacks, and unassigned work.
  4. Proposal storage is inherent. Every proposal is a typed document in MongoDB with version history — no more scavenger hunts in folders.

For the deeper sales-workflow walkthrough — customer archetypes, Indian conventions, vendor and OEM landscape — see business-context.md. For the locked M1 scope and the success criteria the project is held to, see milestone-1.html.

7Functional requirements

Grouped by user role. Each story has acceptance criteria. Status badges flag what's already shipped in M1.

BD Head stories

As a BD Head Shipped
I want to see every active lead in one place, colour-coded by whether I've assigned it yet, so I can triage at a glance.
Acceptance: New Deals for Assignment rail on dashboard shows all non-converted leads. White card = unassigned. Green card = assigned. Cards I've assigned visibly persist (green) until the BD picks them up.
As a BD Head Shipped
I want to assign a lead to one or more BDs from the card, so I don't have to switch tools to delegate.
Acceptance: Clicking a card opens a modal with toggleable team-member chips. Save persists the assignment, stamps assignedAt, and updates the rail card colour and Activity sidebar in real time.
As a BD Head Shipped
I want a team-wide activity surface so I can spot stalled work, expiring proposals, and recent wins without filtering manually.
Acceptance: Right-side Activity sidebar shows team-wide sections — Unassigned leads · Recently assigned · Team in progress · Awaiting reply · Expiring soon · Stalled · Won recently.

BD stories

As a BD Shipped
I want to see only the new assignments I need to act on, at the top of my dashboard, so I don't drown in team-wide noise.
Acceptance: "Your new assignments" rail on the BD's dashboard shows only non-converted leads where my id is in assignedTo[]. All cards are green-tinted.
As a BD Shipped
I want to read everything about a lead — telecaller conversation, attached documents, site imagery — and then start a proposal in one click.
Acceptance: LeadDetailModal exposes tabbed Main / Notes / Media / Uploaded views + an embedded AI chat panel. "Go to Proposal" closes the modal, flips converted: true, and opens the new-proposal wizard.
As a BD Pending M2
I want to edit the proposal in natural language ("change advance to 15%, OMS to 5 years"), and have the PDF re-render automatically.
Acceptance: Chat panel in the proposal editor accepts NL input; the AI service interprets it as a tool call, the block model is updated, and the PDF preview refreshes within 30 seconds.
As a BD Pending M2
I want one button to re-cost an expiring proposal against today's prices.
Acceptance: "Re-cost" button on the proposal triggers the pricing service. The proposal's variables are updated, the PDF is re-rendered, and the revision is incremented.
As a BD Shipped
I want a working notebook attached to each proposal — my own text notes, Excel files, site photos — that survives across sessions.
Acceptance: BD Notes tab in the proposal editor is a unified timeline of entries (text + any combination of attached files / images). Posts persist (M1: localStorage; M2: backend).

Shared / system stories

As any user Shipped
I want my role to be detected at login so the UI tailors itself to my work, without me picking a mode.
Acceptance: Login form routes by email pattern — rajiv... → BD Head, anything else → BD. M2 replaces this with JWT-driven role from the auth endpoint.
As any user Pending M2
I want a Watchdog agent to surface stale leads and expiring proposals without me having to ask, so nothing falls through.
Acceptance: Scheduled job (node-cron) scans daily and writes alerts that surface in the Activity sidebar.

9System architecture

The boxes, the arrows, and where each chunk of logic lives.

Re-Votex is a monolithic Node service for M1.5 — one process serves the API, runs Puppeteer for PDF rendering, talks to Anthropic Claude for AI tool-calls, and exposes scheduled jobs via node-cron. We split a separate PDF worker only if and when rendering becomes a real bottleneck (post-M2). The React frontend is served by the same Express process to keep the deploy story simple. MongoDB holds structured data; S3 holds binary uploads. Hosting is a single EC2 instance.

10Proposal-editing flow

How a chat message turns into a structured update and a re-rendered PDF.

The key invariant: the AI never produces PDF prose directly. It only emits tool calls against a fixed schema of operations (update_var, update_block, add_clause, etc.). The renderer is deterministic — the same proposal document always produces the same PDF. This is the "structured data → PDF, never freeform LLM blob" principle from CLAUDE.md.

15Risks & assumptions

Risks

RiskLikelihoodMitigation
Pixel-faithful PDF rendering diverges from Thermax referenceMediumVisual diffs against the reference PDF on every template change; keep Puppeteer's Chrome version pinned
Anthropic API rate limits or outages break the chat-edit flowLow-mediumPrompt caching reduces cost / call volume; degrade gracefully to a "system unavailable" UI; queue retries
Single EC2 instance fails during business hoursLowDaily MongoDB snapshots to S3; UptimeRobot alert; recovery within 4 hours; multi-AZ in Phase 3
Freshsales API changes break the syncLowSync runs in a separate module with its own retry/backoff and clear error reporting; manual fallback always available
Real price data is slow to land — re-cost still feels manualMediumAdmin-friendly price-sheet UI in M2; live feeds (NSE, OPIS) Phase 3
Solo / lean team can't keep up with feature requestsHighStrict M1 → M1.5 → M2 phasing; out-of-scope list defended; design system locked so visuals don't sprawl
Roles in real use are richer than just BD / BD Head (e.g., legal, finance)MediumJWT-driven role model accepts arbitrary role strings; UI gating is centralised in a single map for cheap extension

Assumptions

  • Sorigin's deal volume stays in the hundreds-per-year range. Sizing for thousands would push us toward multi-instance hosting and a job queue.
  • Sorigin will continue to use Freshsales as the lead-management CRM of record. Re-Votex never tries to replace it.
  • The Thermax-style PDF format is acceptable as the canonical Sorigin format. If a major rebrand happens, the template changes but the architecture doesn't.
  • JavaScript on both sides of the wire remains the team's preferred language. TypeScript was considered and explicitly declined for the lean-team posture.
  • MongoDB's schemaless nature is acceptable for proposal documents whose shape varies by vertical. Migrations are versioned but light.

Explicitly out of scope

  • Multi-user / multi-tenant. Re-Votex is single-tenant inside Sorigin. White-labelling for other EPCs is not on the roadmap.
  • Replacing Freshsales as the CRM. Lead lifecycle inside Re-Votex starts post-handoff.
  • Document OCR. Electricity-bill data extraction is Phase 2 at earliest.
  • Real-time price feeds. Manual price-sheet entry in M2; automated feeds Phase 3.
  • Wind / BESS / hybrid verticals. Architecture is plug-ready; only solar ships in M1.
  • Mobile app. Phase 3+ at earliest.
  • Investor / pitch-deck slides. Distinct artefact; not a BRD concern.
